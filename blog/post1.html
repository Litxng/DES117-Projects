The Three Layers of the Web

2025-06-25

There are 3 parts of the web: the surface web, the deep web, and the dark web. The surface web is easily searchable and is open to the public. It includes websites like Google, YouTube, and CNN.

The deep web refers to websites that aren’t searchable and require the exact URL or login credentials to access them. This includes items such as emails and bank logins.

The dark web is only accessible with a specific browser called Tor, and users can browse anonymously. The dark web began in 2000 with the release of Freenet by a student, Ian Clarke. Clarke wanted to create a new way to anonymously communicate and share files online. This kick-started the Tor project, which was released in 2002 and launched a browser in 2008. With Tor, users can now browse the internet completely anonymously.

Originally, Tor was used by the United States Department of Defense to communicate anonymously, but the dark web has now become a place for users who want to stay anonymous around the world. The dark web uses a technology called “onion routing” that protects users from being traced.

With that technology, people use the dark web for illegal purposes like selling drugs, data, trading/distributing illegal pornography, and other harmful acts.

To access the dark web, you first need to download Tor. To get into dark web websites, you will need the exact URL, so select a dark web search engine like DuckDuckGo that can help you find dark web websites that can’t be traced by traditional surface web search engines. Dark web websites have a .onion URL. Finally, make sure to have a VPN and strong antimalware software to prevent illegal attacks.

I have never tried to go into the dark web since I am too scared of getting doxxed. I remember watching scary animated story videos of people going into the dark web and getting into websites that they shouldn’t be getting into, and then getting killed or something.

Although the dark web is known to be dangerous and illegal, I feel like the surface web is just as dangerous. Since I pretty much grew up on the internet, looking back on it, I had no cyber awareness at all, and I am sure that is the case for a lot of young people out there.

I have met a lot of weirdos that I blindly trusted. I even tried Skype calling my “online friends” and accidentally turned my camera on. With the internet being built into our daily lives, I think cybersecurity should be taught to young people.
